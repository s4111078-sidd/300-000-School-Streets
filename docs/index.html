About this project

300,000 Streets of Melbourne — a Regen Melbourne × RMIT University initiative

This tool assesses the pedestrian and cyclist safety of walking routes around secondary schools in Melbourne using the Healthy Streets framework — a 10-indicator system developed by Lucy Saunders and adopted by Transport for London.

Each school gate is scored across 10 indicators (0 = worst, 10 = best) using a combination of field observation, OpenStreetMap data, EPA air quality readings, VicRoads crash records, and ABS Census data. A score of 6.0 or above across all indicators is the target threshold for a safe school street.

The equity analysis reveals that the most socioeconomically disadvantaged school catchments (SEIFA Decile 4) also have the worst safety outcomes — a Pearson correlation of r = 0.84 — supporting a prioritised investment approach.

The scenario engine uses a Ridge regression model trained on open data to predict how physical interventions (crossings, bike lanes, footpaths, etc.) would change Healthy Streets scores, helping prioritise spend before committing capital.
